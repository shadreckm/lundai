# LUNDAI — Experience Design Guidelines

"The Warm Heart of Africa — in an AI-Powered Interface"

## 1. Tone & Voice
The LUNDAI platform should feel like a helpful, local guide.

| Element | Tone | Example Message |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Search UI** | Helpful & Friendly | *"Let’s find you a comfortable place near campus today."* |
| **Error Handling** | Patient & Humble | *"Moni! We couldn't find that just yet. Try searching for 'Blantyre' instead?"* |
| **AI Insights**| Proactive & Insightful | *"This home is a great value for LUANAR students!"* |
| **Onboarding** | Welcoming | *"Welcome to LUNDAI — helping you find a home in the Warm Heart of Africa."* |

## 2. Visual Language
-   **Color Palette**: Use warm earth tones (deep oranges, browns) mixed with modern "AI" purples and teals to symbolize the fusion of tradition and technology.
-   **Typography**: Clean sans-serif (Inter/Roboto) for readability on low-resolution mobile screens.
-   **Icons**: Use friendly, rounded icons for "Bed", "Bath", and "WiFi".
-   **Badges**: High-contrast "Verified" badges to build trust in a market with high fraud rates.

## 3. Trust Signals for Malawi
-   **Landlord Verification**: Use the phrase *"Verified Landlord"* prominently.
-   **University Proximity**: Always show distance to the nearest well-known college (e.g., *"3 min walk to Chancellor College"*).
-   **Direct WhatsApp**: The primary CTA should be WhatsApp, as it is the most trusted and commonly used communication tool in Malawi.

## 4. Performance UX (Low Bandwidth)
-   **Progressive Loading**: Show text details before high-res images.
-   **Blurry Image Placeholders**: Ensure the UI doesn't look "broken" while images load.
-   **Offline Caching**: Allow users to save listings when they have WiFi for later offline viewing.

## 5. Community-Focused Features
-   **Roommate Matching**: Emphasize community and shared costs.
-   **Student Reviews**: User-generated content from other students to build a "safe haven" reputation.
-   **Local Landmarks**: Use neighborhoods people actually use (e.g., *"Near Chichiri Mall"*, *"Area 18"*) rather than just street names.
